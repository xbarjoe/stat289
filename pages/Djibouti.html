Djibouti ( Arabic : جيبوتي ‎ Jībūtī ), officially the Republic of Djibouti , is a country in the Horn of Africa . It is bordered by Eritrea in the north, Ethiopia in the west and south, and Somalia in the southeast. The remainder of the border is formed by the Red Sea and the Gulf of Aden . Djibouti, with 865,000 citizens (2009), is one of the least populous countries in Africa. [5] The predominant religion in Djibouti, with a 94% majority, is Islam, with the remaining six percent claiming Christianity. In the 1800s, Djibouti, then French Somaliland, was acquired by France through various treaties with Somali sultans. [6] It was declared an independent nation in 1977, and changed its name to the present day Republic of Djibouti. Djibouti joined the United Nations on September 20, 1977. [7] [8] While Djibouti is an independent state, it maintains deep French relations, and through various military and economic agreements with France, it receives continued security and economic assistance.
