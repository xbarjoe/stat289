Antigua and Barbuda ( / æ n ˈ t iː ɡ ( w ) ə  ...  b ɑːr ˈ b ( j ) uː d ə /  ( listen ) ; ann- TEE -g(w)ə ... bar- B(Y)OO -də ) is a country in the West Indies in the Americas , lying between the Caribbean Sea and the Atlantic Ocean . It consists of two major islands, Antigua and Barbuda , and a number of smaller islands (including Great Bird , Green , Guiana , Long , Maiden and York Islands and further south, the island of Redonda ). The permanent population numbers about 81,800 (at the 2011 Census) and the capital and largest port and city is St. John's on Antigua. Lying near each other (the main Barbuda airport is less than 0.5° of latitude , or 30 nautical miles , north of the main Antigua airport ), Antigua and Barbuda are in the middle of the Leeward Islands , part of the Lesser Antilles , roughly at 17°N of the equator .
