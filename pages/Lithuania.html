Lithuania ( / ˌ l ɪ θj u ˈ eɪ n i ə /  ( listen ) ; [11] Lithuanian : Lietuva [lʲɪɛtʊˈvɐ] ), officially the Republic of Lithuania (Lithuanian: Lietuvos Respublika ), is a country in the Baltic region of northern - eastern Europe . Since its independence, Lithuania has been referred to as one of the Baltic states . It is situated along the southeastern shore of the Baltic Sea , to the east of Sweden and Denmark . It is bordered by Latvia to the north, Belarus to the east and south, Poland to the south, and Kaliningrad Oblast (a Russian exclave ) to the southwest. Lithuania has an estimated population of 2.8 million people as of 2017 [update] , and its capital and largest city is Vilnius . Other major cities are Kaunas and Klaipėda . Lithuanians are Baltic people . The official language, Lithuanian , along with Latvian , is one of only two living languages in the Baltic branch of the Indo-European language family.
